# Brand Guide

## Active References

- Primary source-of-truth mockup: `concepts/concept-2.png`
- Active hero source: `concepts/hero.png`

## Brand Strategy

- Primary goal: move visitors to `CONTACT US`
- Form is priority; phone is secondary
- Communicate premium flooring work, trust, and the next action within 5 seconds
- Positioning: expert floor refinishing with a premium, warm, grounded feel
- Promise: beautiful floors, lasting results, clear path to estimate
- Rule: each section should either build trust or move the visitor toward contact

## Homepage Structure

- Quiet header / minimal navigation
- Large hero with immediate `CONTACT US` CTA
- Exactly 3 value statements
- Examples section with 3x3 image grid
- Bottom contact CTA with fast-response reassurance
- Minimal footer

## Visual Direction

- Warm, premium, editorial, high-contrast
- Bright architectural hero paired with a dark proof/gallery block
- Architecture cues: arches, stairs, halls, curves
- Natural materials and premium residential interiors
- Organic accents are acceptable if restrained

- Light Oak: `#C6A46F`

## Color Rules

- Use `Warm Ivory` or `Plaster Beige` for breathing room
- Use `Gallery Black` or `Espresso` for the proof/gallery zone, not everywhere
- Use `CTA Bronze` for primary buttons and small accents
- Keep accent colors restrained
- Dark sections must still feel luminous through image contrast and CTA visibility

## Typography

- Headlines: editorial serif
- UI, nav, buttons: clean sans-serif
- Body: restrained sans-serif
- Constraint: Google Fonts only
- Recommended stack:
  - Headlines: `Playfair Display` or similar
  - UI / Nav / Buttons: `Montserrat` or `Inter`
  - Body: `Inter`

## Type Rules

- Let headlines do the emotional work
- Keep body copy short
- Keep line lengths tight, especially on mobile

## Copy Direction

- Short, grounded, premium
- Visuals carry the weight; copy guides confidence and action

## Components

- Buttons: rounded rectangle, strong fill, generous tap target
- Cards: subtle radius, light texture, thin borders only when useful
- Value strip: exactly 3 items on homepage
- Icons: thin but legible
- Gallery: high-quality crops, rounded corners, no filler
- Footer: minimal contact path, brand line, essential links only

## Photography Rules

- Use:
  - warm interior light
  - real wood floors
  - arches, stairs, halls
  - potted organic accents
  - contrast-rich gallery photos
  - closeups of herringbone and finish details
- Avoid:
  - generic stock rooms
  - sterile white spaces
  - random decorative lines
  - blobs and waves
  - murky dark sections
  - busy furniture-heavy scenes

## Layout Principles

- Eye flow: brand -> headline -> `CONTACT US` -> trust -> examples -> `CONTACT US`
- Use contrast blocks intentionally: light hero, dark examples, light contact/footer
- Organic shape language should stay architectural
- Mobile-first: no 4-column value strips; 3 is the maximum
- Remove anything that does not build trust or contact intent

## Inner Page System

- Do not repeat the custom homepage hero on inner pages
- Use persistent FSE header plus compact title band
- Keep inner pages simpler, scannable, and contact-forward

## Inner Page Notes

- Services: service cards/list, short descriptions, scope reassurance, CTA
- Experience: gallery-first portfolio, proof-driven
- About: short trust page, no mission fluff
- Contact: form first, phone secondary, service area, response expectation

## Logo / Favicon Direction

- No final full logo yet
- Homepage concept already includes visible `PARADIS FLOORING` brand treatment and should be treated as branded, not unbranded
- Favicon direction: bold sans-serif `PD` monogram
- Use espresso ground with ivory and bronze contrast
- Avoid thin strokes, tiny detail, outlines, and texture

## Avoid

- Corporate mission fluff
- Long paragraphs
- Vague claims without proof
- Too many service details on the homepage
- Anything that distracts from `CONTACT US`

## When To Update

- After concept generation
- After selecting a preferred direction
- When palette, type, spacing, or component style decisions are finalized
